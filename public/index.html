The Challenge

India's Irrigation Crisis

Over 80% of India's freshwater is consumed by agriculture — yet most farmers irrigate based on guesswork, leading to massive waste and poor crop health.

Water Wastage

Traditional flood irrigation wastes up to 60% of water through evaporation and runoff, draining precious groundwater reserves.

Energy Drain

Farmers pump excessive groundwater using electric or diesel motors, resulting in huge electricity bills and carbon emissions.

Low Yield

Over-watering or under-watering directly impacts crop quality, reducing yield by up to 25% and hurting farmer income.

Pro Tips

Irrigation Best Practices

01

Irrigate Early Morning

Water between 6–8 AM to minimize evaporation and maximize absorption.

02

Check Soil Moisture

Insert a finger 2 inches into soil — if dry, it's time to irrigate.

03

Use Drip Irrigation

Drip systems deliver water directly to roots, saving up to 50% water.

04

Mulch Your Fields

A layer of mulch reduces surface evaporation and keeps soil moist longer.
